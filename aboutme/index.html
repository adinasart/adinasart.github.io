About Adina
Short Biography
Adina Cicort is a visual artist with a multicultural personality living in the United States. Born in 1978 in Bucharest Romania and inspired by her mother, a talented self thought artist, she expressed an interest in arts and culture from an early age. Later on, speaking several languages she was able to communicate with people from other countries which contributed to her study of understanding about life and our planet as a whole. Traveling in the Romanian country side from the Black Sea Coast to the Carpathian Mountains, discovered her true calling: to create. She defined her artistic vision under the influence of a famous Romanian artist Daniel Petru Ionescu and other European artists and continued her art journey and education in the United States. Although she paints from the age of three, years later, living with her husband in America, she found the right moment and place to dedicate her life entirely to ART. A high spirituality is revealed in subjects such as: history of civilizations and religions, symbolism, nature, countries, architecture, rituals and traditions. Abstract and folk art styles and mediums such as: oil, acrylic, pastel, colored pencil are favorites. Adina creates as well art for children, book illustrations and graphic design for advertising industry.
 
Artist's Statement
"Being a true artist means to be blessed of receiving such a great gift as creation! God, the supreme positive force that lays in everybody's hearts and minds and all around us, creates within yourself and opens the path to what we call miracles, therefore your duty as an artist is to share and to inspire other's people life!

My artworks are the expression of my feelings, memories and genetic code inherited from my ancestors. They were simple people from the plains of Walachia and the mountains of Transylvania and Moldavia for whom Art was a lifestyle, a joy and a celebration of life and God. I believe in the power of art as therapy and a form of communication thus the existence becomes a journey trough the power of mind, into a universe of energies, colors, sounds and forms. The Art creates the big puzzle."

Exhibitions, Art Shows and Media Coverage
Adina's artworks are held in private collections in several countries in Europe like Romania, France, Italy, Germany and UK, and in the United States. In the media she is often present in the Romanian Diaspora newspapers and radio stations, especially for the American Romanian Community, as well in other art related publications worldwide.
